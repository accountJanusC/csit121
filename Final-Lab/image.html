Final Reflection cont'd

I revisited and modified Lab 4 for the Images webpage here. I spent quite a bit of time trying to find a suitable background image after having the 'background-image: url("");' CSS property prepared, since none of the previous ones I used provided sufficient colour contrast to make text content visible. The handful of movie/animation images I tried had made other elements of the site (like the text) hard to see. So one thing I definitely (re)learned was that a background image should only have one shade of colour for maximum contrast and readability on a website. I also (re)learned from W3Schools that 'margin-left: auto;' + 'margin-right: auto;' are good for centering graphics and other elements in CSS. And like with the previous image-related labs, I needed to use sites like freeconvert.com to convert avif and webp formats to JPG or PNG ones.
